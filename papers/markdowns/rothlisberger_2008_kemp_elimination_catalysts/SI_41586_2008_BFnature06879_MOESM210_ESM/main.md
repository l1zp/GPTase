# Validating the Kemp elimination activity of the designed and evolved enzymes

The possibility of a contaminating enzyme being responsible for the observed catalytic activity was ruled out by several experiments.

a. Possible sources of contamination The Kemp elimination is an unnatural reaction, and it is highly probable that no natural enzyme exists, certainly in E. coli, that catalyzes this reaction as its primary，native function. It could may well be that this reaction is catalyzed by certain enzymes in a promiscuous manner, and hence at low efficiency. However, bacterial lysates of the strains used here for over-expression of the designed templates do not exhibit Kemp elimination activity above the buffer blank. Thus, it is highly improbable that the activity observed with the designed templates is coming from endogenous $E$ ，coli enzyme.Further，to account for the observed activities， the contaminating enzyme will have to dominate the preparation (due to its weak catalytic activity） rather than contaminate it at low percentage, and also co-purify with the designed enzymes throughout the process (see Section c below).

b. The wide range of mutant enzyme activities Site directed mutagenesis of the key catalytic residues was performed on both the designed enzymes and the evolved variants (see Table 1, and Supplementary Table 2). In all the cases, mutating the catalytic general base (either Glu, or His) into Gln or Ala caused a significant reduction (in almost all cases,by well over 1Oo-fold,and in some cases by over l0oo-fold） in the catalytic activity,as measured by in both crude cell lysates and the purified proteins. The designed KE07 was further evolved, and a series of variants carrying up to 8 mutations exhibited catalytic activities that are up to 2O0-fold higher than the designed enzymes. It is unlikely that the levels of a contaminating enzyme(s) that co-purified with the designed enzymes will vary to such degree ${ \it \Omega } > 2 0 0 { \it \Omega }$ -fold up,and ${ \scriptstyle \mathrm { > } } 1 0 0 0$ -fold down), let alone through several purification steps.

c. Protein purification Independent protein purifications were performed using different protocols，and in three different laboratories involved in the project,yet the kinetic parameters of the resulting proteins were essentially identical for all preparations.

B. The designed KEO7,and its evolved variants,were purified in the Department of Biological Chemistry, Weizmann Institute of Science, Rehovot, Israel. C. The KEO7 template and its evolved variants were also purified at the Israel Structural Proteomics Center, Weizmann Institute of Science. # Determination of $\mathbf { k _ { u n c a t } }$ for hydrolysis of ${ \bf 5 - N O } _ { 2 }$ benzisoxazole

$\mathrm { k _ { u n c a t } }$ was determined as a function of buffer concentration at $\mathrm { p H } ~ 7 . 2 5$ at $2 5 \mathrm { { ^ \circ C } }$ and extrapolated to zero buffer concentration.
